To demonstrate the effectiveness and robustness of my approaches, I present several demos that tackle distinct challenges: (i) Multi-Agent Motion Planning (MAMP) in mining transportation, which requires improving loading-area throughput while ensuring safety; (ii) end-to-end navigation, which emphasizes handling complex collision-free tasks under motion constraints; and (iii) racing, which demands highly integrated trajectory planning and control. In addition, I fuse physics into the BEV world model to enhance long-term prediction performance.

Multi-Agent Motion Planning

The Decentralized Multi-Agent Safe Motion Planning framework enables reliable coordination in mining transportation. It decomposes the planning problem into ego reference-line tracking and opponent collision avoidance. A lightweight diffusion model with a U-Net backbone is designed to capture these features, allowing agents to learn waiting and proceeding behaviors that ensure safe multi-agent interactions.

The Physics-Informed World Model is a lightweight generative model that predicts future images conditioned on the current scene and control inputs. By incorporating the relative distance between the ego vehicle and surrounding vehicles via a soft mask as an additional conditioning signal, it effectively captures inter-vehicle interactions, thereby enabling forecasting with strong existential and temporal consistency in dynamic environments.
